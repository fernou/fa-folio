City of Palm Spring GIS Nexus

The City of Palm Springs is composed of dozens of departments and programs. Each department and program are somehow connected and affect one another. This can include project collaboration, permitting and enforcment, or a link within the same workflow chain. The GIS Nexus (what I named it) is an internal application from the ESRI Enterprise application suite. This centeral page allows diffrent departments view information such as zoning, property information, fire hazard area, and dozens more department specfici layers. This helps narrow the lag in communication between departments when figuring out if applicant for a building department falls within a historical district which would trigger a architectural review by the planning department.

Palm Springs International Airport Collection

The Palm Springs International Airport (PSP) is currently undergoing a technology change by switching from a legacy system to a cloud-based assest managment system. GIS is one of the core fundamentals of the assest managment program. PSP has never really used GIS or harnessed its technology to their advantage. At this time the I have been tasked in coordinating the data collection efforts starting with the perimeter. Once the perimeter is collected the interior of the airport will be the next step in data collection. One of the more difficult aspects of this project will be coordinating with the air traffic control tower when we would be able to collect data on the actual runway. Communications of the status of this data collection between the Airport Director and GIS staff is important and it is why I created a dashboard to accomplish this.

Glass Fire - Napa County

On September 27th, 2020, the Glass Fire began burning in the area of Sonoma County and Napa County located in Northern California. Both counties were overwhelemed as they battled the Complex Fire which burned from August 16th, 2020 to November 12th, 2020. Staff resources were not only streched thin but staff were working continuously 24 hours. Napa County broadcasted a Emergency Managers Mutual-Aid (EMMA) request to the geographic area. Within minutes of recieving this request, I volunteered to provide relief to the GIS team.

My duties included creating evacuations maps, provide damage reports to staff, and update road closure and incident maps as needed. On October 20th, 2020, the Glass Fire was 100% contained and all efforts could now go towards the Complex Fire. This was a very stressful situation for me, but a very rewarding feeling knowing that I able provided my services to the public and staff. I will certainly volunteer the next oportunity I get.
